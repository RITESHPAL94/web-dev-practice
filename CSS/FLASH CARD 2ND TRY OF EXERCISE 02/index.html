SUPRA MK4
Toyota M Series

Toyota Supra MK4, or fourth-generation Supra, is a sports car that was produced from 1993 to 1998 in North America and until 2002 in Japan. Hereare some of the features of the MK4 Supra: @EngineThe MK4 Supra had two engine options: a 3.0-liter inline six-cylinder with 220 horsepower and a turbo 3.0- liter inline six with 320 horsepower. The twin-turbo engine was fitted with a six- speed manual gearbox, while the naturally aspirated engine had a five-speed manual gearbox.WeightThe MK4 Supra was lighter than the previous model, with a weight reduction of around 200 pounds. This was achieved by using aluminum for the hood, suspension arms, and transmission pans, and a magnesium steering wheel and plastic gas tank.PerformanceThe MK4 Supra was a serious performer and was one of the fastest in its segment. The naturally aspirated engine could accelerate from 0 to 60 miles per hour in 6.5 seconds with a manual transmission and 7.3 seconds with an automatic transmission.Success in motorsportThe MK4 Supra was very successful in motorsport, winning its class in the Swiss Mountain Races, competing in the Le Mans 24hr race, and being a dominant force in the All-Japan GT Championships.PopularityThe MK4 Supra was a favorite with pro motorsports teams and aftermarket buyers. The 2JZGTE engine was so popular that it could be tuned to over 280 horsepower without affecting reliability.
